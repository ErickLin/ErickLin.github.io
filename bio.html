Bio

My interests lie in pattern recognition and machine learning algorithms. Recently, I was selected to present my research on Markov regime switching models for human pattern recognition at the National Collegiate Research Conference at Harvard. Look under Projects to find out more about my research.

I have experience in Java, C/C++, and Javascript, as well as UNIX/Linux. I fuse practicality and idealism in my work, and my motto is that for anything I don't know, I can learn.

My hobbies include building applications, participating in programming competitions, running, swimming, thinking deeply about questions and issues, and watching movies.

Mission Statement: To obtain a research or software engineering internship in the summer of 2015, to graduate with a Bachelors degree in computer science by May 2018, and to pursue a Ph.D. in computer science
